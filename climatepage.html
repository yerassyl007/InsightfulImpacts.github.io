refers to long-term shifts in Earth's temperatures and weather patterns. While these shifts can occur naturally, human activities have been the primary driver of climate change since the 1800s.

The Greenhouse Effect

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that warms the Earth's surface, but human activities have enhanced this effect dramatically.

Burning Fossil Fuels

The process where greenhouse gases trap heat in Earth's atmosphere, maintaining temperatures suitable for life, it's primary cause is the burning of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and gas), which releases heat-trapping greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Enhanced Greenhouse Effect

The accumulation of greenhouse gases leads to an enhanced greenhouse effect, trapping more heat from the sun and causing global temperatures to rise. Human activities, particularly burning fossil fuels, have increased greenhouse gas concentrations, leading to excessive warming:

  • Carbon dioxide from fossil fuels
  • Methane from agriculture and waste
  • Nitrous oxide from fertilizers
  • Industrial gases (CFCs, HFCs)

Changing Precipitation

Rainfall patterns are shifting, leading to more extreme weather events such as droughts, floods, and hurricanes.

Melting glaciers and ice sheets

The Earth's ice is melting at an unprecedented rate, contributing to sea-level rise.

Ocean Acidification

The oceans are becoming more acidic due to increased carbon dioxide absorption, threatening marine ecosystems.
