Introduction

Both women and men have played crucial roles in advancing climate science, policy, and activism. This page highlights remarkable individuals who have made significant contributions to the fight against climate change, showcasing their unique approaches and lasting impacts on global environmental efforts.

Christiana Figueres (Costa Rica)

UN Climate Change Executive Secretary

Christiana Figueres is a Costa Rican diplomat who served as the Executive Secretary of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC) from 2010 to 2016. She played a pivotal role in the successful negotiation of the Paris Agreement in 2015.

Key Achievements

  • Led negotiations that resulted in the Paris Agreement in 2015
  • Orchestrated cooperation among countries to solve climate change problems
  • Transformed the global climate change negotiations
  • Co-founder of Global Optimism

Wangari Maathai (Kenya)

Founder of the Green Belt Movement

Professor Wangari Maathai was a Kenyan environmental and political activist. She founded the Green Belt Movement, an environmental NGO focused on planting trees, environmental conservation, and improving the living conditions of women in Kenya.

Key Achievements

  • First African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize (2004)
  • Founded the Green Belt Movement, focusing on tree planting and environmental conservation
  • Improved living conditions for women in Kenya through environmental initiatives
  • Advocated for democracy, women's rights, and environmental conservation

Mary Robinson (Ireland)

Climate Justice Advocate

Mary Robinson is an Irish independent politician who served as the first female President of Ireland and the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights. She has been a prominent advocate for climate justice, focusing on how climate change affects vulnerable populations.

Greta Thunberg (Sweden)

Youth Climate Activist

Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist who has gained international recognition for challenging world leaders to take immediate action against climate change. She has become a leading voice for youth climate activism worldwide.

Key Achievements

  • Initiated and leads the global "Fridays for Future" movement
  • Addressed world leaders at the UN Climate Action Summit
  • Named Time Person of the Year in 2019
  • Authored "The Climate Book," gathering opinions from activists, scientists, and experts about the current climate crisis
